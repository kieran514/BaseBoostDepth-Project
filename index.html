BaseBoostDepth: Exploiting Larger Baselines for Self-Supervised Monocular Depth Estimation

Kieran Saunders1, Luis Manso1 George Vogiatzis2,
Aston University1, Loughborough University2
Code arXiv

Abstract

In the domain of multi-baseline stereo, the conventional understanding is that, in general, increasing baseline separation substantially enhances the accuracy of depth estimation. However, prevailing self-supervised depth estimation architectures primarily use minimal frame separation and a constrained stereo baseline. Larger frame separations can be employed; however, we show this to result in diminished depth quality due to various factors, including significant changes in brightness, and increased areas of occlusion. In response to these challenges, our proposed method, BaseBoostDepth, incorporates a curriculum learning-inspired warm-up and boosting optimization strategy to effectively leverage larger frame separations. However, we show that our optimization strategy alone does not suffice, as larger baselines still cause pose estimation drifts, yet they paradoxically enhance foreground detail in the depth network. Leveraging this phenomenon, we introduce partial incremental pose and artificial pose reconstructions, significantly improving all metrics. Furthermore, our boosting phase offers training flexibility; it can be initialized either from the warm-up phase or with pre-trained weights. Ultimately, our final depth network achieves state-of-the-art performance on KITTI and SYNS-patches datasets across image-based, edge-based, and point cloud-based metrics.

Method

We present BaseBoostDepth, a two-stage optimization strategy capable of accurately estimating depth with clearly defined object boundaries. Unlike previous methods, we effectively exploit wider baselines and observe greater brightness-contrast cues, resulting in SotA depth estimations. Enhanced object boundaries are achieved through our boosting stage, which is agnostic to the depth backbone, enabling any pretrained self-supervised model to be boosted and attain improved object boundary definition. Results on KITTI Eigen

We achieve state-of-the-art results on the KITTI test set when using the SQLdepth backbone at a resolution of 640x192, utilizing only one frame at inference with no post-processing. Our method, BaseBoostDepth, employs the same backbone as Monodepth2 and commences training from ImageNet pre-trained weights. On the other hand, $\dagger$ utilizes MonoViT's backbone, starting from MonoViT's pre-trained weights, and $*$ employs SQLdepth's depth backbone, also starting from MonoViT's pre-trained weights. Both of these versions commence training from the boosting stage.

Results on SYNS

We also achieve state-of-the-art results on the SYNS test set when using MonoViT's backbone at a resolution of 640x192, using only one frame at inference with no post-processing.
